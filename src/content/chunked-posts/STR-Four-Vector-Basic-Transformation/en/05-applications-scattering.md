## Applications — Scattering Processes

### Rutherford Scattering

The scattering of charged particles can be analyzed using four-vector methods. For a particle with initial four-momentum $P_i$ scattering off a stationary target, the final four-momentum $P_f$ satisfies

$$P_f = P_i + Q$$

where $Q$ is the four-momentum transfer. The invariant quantity

$$t = (P_f - P_i)^2 = Q^2$$

characterizes the "hardness" of the scattering. For elastic scattering, $t$ is directly related to the scattering angle.

### Cross-Sections in Covariant Form

The differential cross-section for scattering processes can be expressed in Lorentz-invariant form:

$$\frac{d\sigma}{dt} = \frac{1}{64\pi s |\mathbf{p}_i|^2} |\mathcal{M}|^2$$

where $\mathcal{M}$ is the invariant matrix element, $s$ is the Mandelstam variable for the center-of-mass energy squared, and $\mathbf{p}_i$ is the initial three-momentum in the center-of-mass frame.

### Relativistic Invariants in Scattering

The three Mandelstam variables $s$, $t$, $u$ satisfy

$$s + t + u = \sum_i m_i^2 c^2$$

This relation holds for any $2 \to 2$ scattering process and provides a useful check on calculations. The physical region for scattering is determined by the condition that all external particles are on their mass shells $p^2 = m^2 c^2$.

These four-vector methods provide a powerful framework for analyzing particle interactions without reference to specific coordinate systems, fully respecting the principles of special relativity.
