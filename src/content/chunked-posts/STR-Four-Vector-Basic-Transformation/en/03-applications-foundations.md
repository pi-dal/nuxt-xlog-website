## Applications — Decay Processes and Invariant Mass

### Particle Decay

One important application is particle decay: $A \to B + C$. Using four-momentum conservation

$$P_A = P_B + P_C$$

and the invariant $P^2 = m^2 c^2$ (where $m$ is the rest mass), we can derive relations between the energies and momenta of decay products without specifying reference frames.

### Invariant Mass

The invariant mass of a system is defined as

$$M^2 c^2 = \left(\sum_i E_i\right)^2 - \left(\sum_i \mathbf{p}_i c\right)^2$$

This quantity is Lorentz invariant and equals the rest mass of the system in its center-of-mass frame. For a decay process, the invariant mass of the parent particle is conserved.

### Threshold Energy

For reactions like $A + B \to C + D$, the threshold energy for particle production can be elegantly calculated using four-vector invariants. The minimum energy required is found when all final particles are at rest in the center-of-mass frame, giving

$$E_{threshold} = \frac{(m_C + m_D)^2 c^2 - (m_A + m_B)^2 c^2}{2 m_B}$$

This approach avoids complicated frame transformations and directly yields the correct result.
