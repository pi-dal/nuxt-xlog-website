  We have all faced, are facing, or will face the illness or death of a loved
  one. How should we deal with a loss that is destined to be unstoppable? Irving
  Yalom, the master of existential psychotherapy, is famous for treating death
  anxiety. However, when he learned that his beloved wife of sixty years had
  cancer and did not have long to live, he was unable to bear it. When the
  therapist becomes the client, how to fight against despair? How to spend the
  last days of your life meaningfully? This book is a deathbed gift from Mr. and
  Mrs. Yalong to each other. It is the master’s personal experience of
  widowhood, loneliness and mourning. It is also a reflection of his care for
  all living beings. With sincere records and awareness, it reveals the meaning
  of life, death, love and existence, and brings a touch of comfort to people
  who are suffering together.
