  "The Courage of Happiness" is the second and final volume of the "Two Parts of
  Courage". It is also based on the thought of Adler, a psychologist from a
  hundred years ago, and is presented in the form of a story of "Dialogue
  between a Youth and a Philosopher". Three years after the last conversation,
  the young man had quit his job as a librarian and became a primary school
  teacher. But when he appeared in front of the philosopher again, he
  experienced many ravages of reality (at work) and despaired of his original
  ideal: "Adler's thoughts have no meaning in the outside world." The "strong
  medicine" level philosophical dialogue began again... If "The Courage to Be
  Disliked" is a map, "The Courage to Be Happy" is a guide to action. "The
  Courage to Be Disliked" explores "What should we do to be free?" "The Courage
  of Happiness" explores "What should we do to become happy?" Three years after
  the last conversation, the young man has quit his job as a librarian and
  became a primary school teacher. But when he appeared in front of the
  philosopher again, he experienced many ravages of reality (in educational
  work) and despaired of his original ideal. "Adler's thoughts have no meaning
  in the outside world." "You have misunderstandings about Adler's thoughts."
