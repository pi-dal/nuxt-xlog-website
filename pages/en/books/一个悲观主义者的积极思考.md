summary: >-
  "I Read" Series: Positive Thinking of a Pessimist" is taken from
  Schopenhauer's last great work in his life, "Appendices and Supplements",
  which is his supplement and explanation to his philosophical work "The World
  as Will and Representation". This "Appendix and Supplement", written in
  aphoristic style, made Schopenhauer, whose reputation had been unrecognized
  for more than sixty years, become famous overnight. Just as he said, I am now
  standing at the end of the road, my old head unable to bear the laurel wreath.
  ""I Read" Series: Positive Thinking of a Pessimist" is the most essential part
  of "Appendices and Supplements". In the book, Schopenhauer integrates the
  mainstream of Eastern and Western philosophy, reveals human nature and
  sophistication, and uses wise and vivid writing to discuss with us how to
  behave in the world, how to seek happiness and success, in order to make the
  most of this life.
