In this special summer before high school, I went to Hunan with my grandparents and embarked on a pretty interesting journey.

# Day 1

On the first day, we started from home and arrived at Guangzhou South Station, where we spent a very boring two hours. Then we took the high-speed rail to Changsha and settled in. We stayed at the "Conley Hotel." That night's impression was that the portions in Changsha are enormous — a stark contrast to the small-dish culture of Guangzhou.

![83F91C07-F837-4BBC-9E68-0B3361A206AB.jpeg](/article-assets/2022-Hunan-Travelling/83F91C07-F837-4BBC-9E68-0B3361A206AB.jpeg)

# Day 2

Early on the second day, we boarded the tour bus with our packed breakfast, officially beginning our Hunan journey.

On the bus, the guide shared many of Hunan's customs and historical sites. After a steady stream of knowledge, we arrived at Shaoshan — where the "red sun" rose. When people mention Shaoshan, they think of one person: the great leader Mao Zedong. We visited Mao's former residence, learned about his family background and the feng shui of his front yard, had a "Mao family meal," and laid a wreath.

# Day 3

The main attraction of the third day was Tianmen Mountain.

> **Tianmen Mountain** is located in Yongding District, Zhangjiajie, Hunan. Named after the natural wonder Tianmen Cave (Heaven's Gate), it is known as the "First Holy Mountain of Western Hunan."

Along the way, we saw the magnificent scenery. The guide told us about the unsolved mystery of "Tianmen's rotation."

![2CF523A1-12CC-4722-ADB4-0A27BF88C501.jpeg](/article-assets/2022-Hunan-Travelling/2CF523A1-12CC-4722-ADB4-0A27BF88C501.jpeg)

At the base, we took Cable Car Route B up to the mountainside.

![2C1E7DB1-338A-435E-B13A-D97485219C3F.jpeg](/article-assets/2022-Hunan-Travelling/2C1E7DB1-338A-435E-B13A-D97485219C3F.jpeg)

Further up, we reached the entrance of Tianmen Cave.

![0F16AC0B-D0E9-4493-BD35-47D8D2C4F799.jpeg](/article-assets/2022-Hunan-Travelling/0F16AC0B-D0E9-4493-BD35-47D8D2C4F799.jpeg)

At the summit, we walked across the glass-bottomed bridge (acrophobia warning ⚠️).

![0231F758-A01E-4F38-A0B5-138399052902.jpeg](/article-assets/2022-Hunan-Travelling/0231F758-A01E-4F38-A0B5-138399052902.jpeg)

After visiting Tianmen Mountain, we took Cable Car Route A back down to the city (the one that takes over 20 minutes). Then we headed to Huangshizhai in Zhangjiajie National Forest Park, where we watched the sunset.

![3A629F46-6000-4AD9-8556-735C17D37946.jpeg](/article-assets/2022-Hunan-Travelling/3A629F46-6000-4AD9-8556-735C17D37946.jpeg)

# Day 4

Early on the fourth day, we took a bus to Yuanjiajie. We saw a beautiful reservoir from the scenic bus (unfortunately I didn't take a photo 🤤🤤🤤). Then began a 3+ hour hike.

![CA4ED71D-9B09-4EDC-BA7D-50EEE6D2DDEE.jpeg](/article-assets/2022-Hunan-Travelling/CA4ED71D-9B09-4EDC-BA7D-50EEE6D2DDEE.jpeg)

In the afternoon, we visited Huanglong Cave, but since I'd been to many karst caves before, it wasn't very surprising.

# Day 5

On the fifth day, we visited the Tusi City and learned about the history of the Tujia ethnic group.

![514A677F-3DE1-4CE2-8C16-1EC4D12239A5.jpeg](/article-assets/2022-Hunan-Travelling/514A677F-3DE1-4CE2-8C16-1EC4D12239A5.jpeg)

In the afternoon, we took a bus to Fenghuang Ancient Town.

![3FF8C15A-6437-4D64-BEE9-A87DC56F3004.jpeg](/article-assets/2022-Hunan-Travelling/3FF8C15A-6437-4D64-BEE9-A87DC56F3004.jpeg)

# Day 6

The last day of the trip was very relaxed. In the morning, we visited a local cultural center in Fenghuang, where I met designer An Jie (an amazing person — I hope she successfully opens her own studio). Then we visited the Miao village of Song Zuying's grandmother.

![41C6F9F9-9D57-4ED8-9028-4707AF2F779F.jpeg](/article-assets/2022-Hunan-Travelling/41C6F9F9-9D57-4ED8-9028-4707AF2F779F.jpeg)

In the afternoon, we returned to Changsha by bus, saw the "super moon," passed by "National University of Defense Technology," and returned to the hotel from the first night.

![F517C511-63FA-487F-8961-A5CA30B84241.jpeg](/article-assets/2022-Hunan-Travelling/F517C511-63FA-487F-8961-A5CA30B84241.jpeg)

# Day 7

Early on the seventh day, we took the high-speed rail back to Guangzhou.

# Behind the Scenes

This trip happened right before the high school entrance exam results were released. The education bureau originally said results would be out on the 18th, but on Day 5 they suddenly moved it up to the 15th, and on Day 6 they said it would be early to 2 PM on the 14th (Day 7). This made me quite nervous the last two days, and I couldn't return to school on time to pick up my diploma.

I traveled with my grandparents on this trip. Since they're elderly, I had to handle everything, which left me exhausted (reminiscent of exam season). But it was absolutely worth it:

1. I took my grandfather to visit Chairman Mao's former residence, somewhere he'd always wanted to go
2. I showed my grandmother the beautiful landscapes she'd never seen before
3. I learned about the poverty in Western Hunan and how tourism drives economic development
4. ...

Finally, I hope everyone can visit Hunan, see the beautiful landscapes of our country, and help drive local economic development. And I hope the pandemic ends soon so we can travel freely again 💪💪💪.
