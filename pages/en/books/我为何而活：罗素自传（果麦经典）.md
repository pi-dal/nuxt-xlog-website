summary: >-
  Russell was a famous philosopher and mathematician, known as the "Prophet of
  the Century". He made achievements in many fields other than philosophy and
  won the Nobel Prize for Literature in 1950. He was one of the most influential
  social activists in the West in the 20th century and an advocate and organizer
  of the world peace movement. He also came to China to give lectures and had a
  profound influence on the cultural enlightenment and social reform of modern
  China. Russell passed away at the age of 98. He fully reviewed his
  100-year-old life in "Why I Live: The Autobiography of Russell". "There are
  three emotions, pure and passionate, that dominate my life: the desire for
  love, the pursuit of knowledge, and the unstoppable sympathy for human
  suffering." The story slowly unfolds around the three driving forces that
  support the boat of his life. Russell's life was a perfect fusion of reason
  and passion: he achieved a prominent position, climbed to the pinnacle of
  knowledge, had emotional happiness, experienced prison, and finally lived a
  happy life. Reading "Why I Live: The Autobiography of Russell", we can enter
  the inner world of this great thinker and feel his firm belief in pursuing
  truth and justice. At the same time, we can also think about our own life
  aspirations and choices, and know how to find our own path to happiness.
