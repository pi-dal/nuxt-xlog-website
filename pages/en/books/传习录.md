  "Zhuan Xi Lu" is a collection of quotations and theoretical letters written by
  Wang Yangming, a Chinese philosopher from the Ming Dynasty and a
  representative of the Taoist central school of Song and Ming Dynasties. "Chuan
  Xi" comes from the phrase "Chuan Xi" in "The Analects of Confucius". This book
  provides a comprehensive and detailed interpretation of the original text of
  "Zhuan Xi Lu". It not only covers the theoretical framework and translation of
  the original book, but also analyzes its original text in the subsequent
  "interpretation" while identifying its essence and mystery, exploring its
  academic origins and cultural background, allowing readers to gain more wisdom
  in life while reading.
