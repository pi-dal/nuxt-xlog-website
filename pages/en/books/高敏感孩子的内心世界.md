summary: >-
  If your child: often feels that the sound is too loud, the light is too
  strong, and the sweater is too knotty; is good at catching the emotional
  changes of people around him and is easily influenced; one idea can emanate
  ten new ideas; is overly concerned about other people's opinions and
  evaluations; likes to hide in corners and play by himself; asks questions that
  are surprisingly mature and sometimes very childish; desires more attention;
  often worries and does things for others, and is overly involved... This shows
  that he has a trait - high sensitivity. High sensitivity is not a flaw, but an
  innate talent. The author of this book, Christe Butikonen, has decades of
  experience in dealing with highly sensitive people. She believes that highly
  sensitive children are often labeled with various "deficiencies" and they are
  misunderstood and ignored. The best way to help these children is to
  understand and accept their characteristics, and use positive guidance to
  stimulate the children's innate strengths. This book also has clear methods
  for parents on how to grow with their highly sensitive children and help them
  navigate school successfully.
